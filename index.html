FAQ

Q1: Why is my Wi-Fi slow even with high-speed internet?
A: It could be due to poor router placement, interference from other devices, or outdated equipment.

Q2: Where should I place my Wi-Fi router?
A: Place it in the center of your home, elevated, and away from walls, microwaves, or TVs.

Q3: How can I improve my Wi-Fi speed at home?
A: Update router firmware, switch to a modern router (Wi-Fi 6/6E), use mesh systems, or hardwire heavy devices.

Q4: Why does my Wi-Fi drop frequently?
A: This may happen due to interference, overheating, or too many connected devices. Restarting or upgrading the router often helps.

Q5: Is 2.4 GHz or 5 GHz better for Wi-Fi?
A: 2.4 GHz covers longer distances but is slower, while 5 GHz is faster with less range—best for high-speed needs nearby.
